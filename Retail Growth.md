> **Is our success abroad truly scalable, or is it high-risk?**

Rather than reporting historical performance, this analysis functions as a **Growth Audit**.  
It identifies **where value is created**, **where it leaks**, and **where scaling is structurally safe**.

## Page 1 ‚Äî Business Performance Pulse (The Health Check)

**Core Question:**  
Is our current success diversified and stable, or concentrated and fragile?

**Key Findings:**
- **Revenue concentration risk:** 51% of total revenue is generated by just **263 customers**
- **Category dependency:** Home Decor contributes over **35% of total revenue**
- **April anomaly:** A ~25% revenue drop in Q2 initially appears demand-driven

**Critical Insight:**  
Order volume remained stable during April. Revenue fell due to **fulfillment failure**, not customer disengagement ‚Äî indicating **operational fragility**, not market weakness.

**Why This Matters:**  
Before scaling internationally, leadership must understand whether revenue strength is **structural or accidental**.

## Page 2 ‚Äî Customer Insights & VIP Analysis (The ‚ÄúWhale‚Äù Strategy)

**Core Question:**  
Who is actually funding the business ‚Äî and are they loyal?

**Key Findings:**
- Identified a **VIP tier (~top 5%)** with:
  - Average annual spend of **~$27K per customer**
  - Disproportionate contribution to total revenue
- **Netherlands** emerges as the most efficient international market:
  - ~70% repeat purchase rate
  - High Average Order Value (**~$2.8K**)

**Critical Insight:**  
Not all revenue is equal. Retained, high-frequency customers generate **compounding value** with lower risk than new acquisition.

## Page 3 ‚Äî Operational Efficiency & Diagnostics (The Leaky Bucket)

**Core Question:**  
Where is revenue being lost before it ever hits the bank?

**Key Findings:**
- Identified **~$0.6M in recoverable revenue leakage** driven by cancellations
- Leakage is **concentrated, not systemic**:
  - Spain‚Äôs *Storage & Organization* category shows **~36% leakage**
- High order volume persists in affected months ‚Äî confirming demand exists

**Critical Insight:**  
International growth is **not constrained by demand or marketing**.  
It is constrained by **operational ceilings** that silently erode revenue.

## Page 4 ‚Äî Strategic Growth & Forecasting (The Path Forward)

**Core Question:**  
If we fix friction and protect loyalty, what does growth realistically look like?

**Key Findings:**
- Time-series forecasting shows:
  - **Upper-bound monthly revenue** stabilizing near **$900K**
  - Lower bound reflects inaction on operational issues
- A **30% retention stability threshold** is required for sustainable growth
- Home Decor and Kitchen & Dining show positive MoM momentum in high-efficiency markets

**Critical Insight:**  
Growth is achievable ‚Äî **but only if operational leaks are sealed before scale is attempted**.

## üõ†Ô∏è Technical Implementation & Engineering

### 1. Semantic Layer & Category Mapping
- Engineered a **priority-based keyword mapping table** to classify unstructured product descriptions
- Applied consistent business logic across **540K+ transactions**
- Reduced ‚ÄúOther‚Äù category noise to statistically insignificant levels

**Why it matters:**  
Separates business logic from measures, improves auditability, and enables scalable category refinement without rewriting DAX.

---

### 2. Advanced DAX & Metrics
- **Net Revenue:** Adjusts for unit price variance and cancellations  
- **Leakage Index:** Quantifies gross-to-net erosion by country and category  
- **VIP LTV:** Lifetime value isolated to the top customer cohort  

All measures remain stable under heavy cross-filtering and page-level context.

### ‚úÖ PROCEED ‚Äî Netherlands & Australia
- Scale immediately

- Focus on **share-of-wallet**, not acquisition volume

### ‚ö†Ô∏è PAUSE ‚Äî Spain & Japan
